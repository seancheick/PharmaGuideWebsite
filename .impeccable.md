## Design Context

### Users

PharmaGuide serves three overlapping audiences — all are equally important:

1. **Health-conscious adults** (30-55) who take 3+ supplements daily and want to optimize their stack safely.
2. **Patients on medications** managing chronic conditions (statins, thyroid meds, blood pressure, blood thinners) who add supplements and need to know what interacts.
3. **Caregivers / parents** managing someone else's regimen — an elderly parent, a child, a partner.

Their context when arriving: they've likely Googled "can I take X with Y" and gotten three conflicting answers. They're not panicking, but they're uncertain. They want clarity without having to become a pharmacist themselves.

### Brand Personality

**Calm. Precise. Trustworthy.**

Like a pharmacist who explains clearly — no drama, just facts. The voice is second-person, restrained, and honest about limits. PharmaGuide admits when evidence is weak rather than projecting false confidence. No exclamation marks. No hype words. Concrete numbers over adjectives.

**Emotional goals (in order of the homepage journey):**
1. "I didn't know this existed" — surprise that the gap is real
2. "This is serious / credible" — confidence in the people behind it
3. "I need this for my stack" — personal urgency from seeing themselves in scenarios
4. "Finally, clarity" — calm reassurance that the noise is over

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** Premium health intelligence. The intersection of Oura Ring's restrained data-forward design and Mayo Clinic's institutional authority. Not a wellness brand. Not a startup. Not a hospital.

**References:**
- Oura Ring / Whoop — premium health tech, dark accents, data-forward, restrained
- Mayo Clinic / UpToDate — medical authority, institutional trust, content-density when needed

**Anti-references (explicitly avoid):**
- Wellness / clean-beauty — no sage green gradients, no leaf icons, no "holistic journey" vibes
- Generic SaaS / startup — no gradient hero blobs, no "trusted by 10,000+ teams" badges, no purple CTAs
- Pharma / hospital — no sterile white-blue, no stock photos of doctors with stethoscopes

**Theme:** Light mode primary (warm off-white #FAF9F6). Dark tokens pre-defined for future toggle. Single brand accent: deep teal (#183B3F). Severity colors are functional, not decorative.

**Typography:** Geist Sans for UI, Newsreader italic for editorial punch lines, Geist Mono for data. Weights capped at 600 — restraint is the premium signal.

**Motion:** Slow, emphasized easing curves. "Apple / Linear / Oura" feel. Never bouncy or fast. Reduced motion fully respected.

### Design Principles

1. **Restraint is the message.** Every visual choice should feel intentional. If it doesn't serve clarity or trust, remove it. No decoration without purpose.

2. **Calm over clever.** Users are making health decisions. The interface should lower anxiety, not raise it. Avoid anything that feels urgent, flashy, or gamified.

3. **Evidence first, opinion never.** The 5-tier severity system, evidence grades, and source citations are the product's credibility. Design should elevate data, not bury it in aesthetics.

4. **Personal, not generic.** The homepage should make visitors see themselves — their medications, their supplements, their timing conflicts. Specificity > abstraction.

5. **Admit limits gracefully.** The design should make disclaimers, weak evidence labels, and "we don't" statements feel like strengths, not legal fine print. Honesty is the brand.

### Accessibility

- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance as the baseline
- `prefers-reduced-motion` globally respected (already implemented)
- Focus-visible rings on all interactive elements
- Severity tiers must communicate meaning through shape + label, not color alone (already implemented with distinct shapes per tier)
- Generous line-heights and readable body sizes (16px base, 15px secondary minimum)
